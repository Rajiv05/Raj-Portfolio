Electrolux - Osmo Watering Can

Unitec - 3rd Year - Semester 1 - 2014

This design was a conceptual project for Electrolux which is a simple indoor air purifier which collects its own water to water indoor plants. The water is also purified so it is safe to drink if needed. It is energy saving because it uses solar power to power the device. Basically the product I have designed is a minimalist mini dehumidifier. There are two rings on the outside of the purifier part and on the inside of the main body, they represent a positive and a negative charge that powers the purifier part using solar power. The solar panels are the circular patterns located on the back of the body. When needed to charge the device place the product on a window sill during the day and water your plants when needed. I got inspiration from water itself to come up with the form. I wanted the design to work in relation to its function which is to collect water. The form represents a water drop that is just about to drip
